How to Use EMI Calculator

What is EMI?

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed amount you pay to the lender each month to repay your loan. It consists of both principal and interest components.

1 Enter Loan Amount

Input the total amount you wish to borrow. You can use the slider or type directly in the input field.

2 Enter Interest Rate

Enter the annual interest rate offered by your lender. This rate determines how much interest you'll pay over the loan term.

3 Enter Loan Tenure

Specify the number of years over which you'll repay the loan. Longer tenures mean lower EMIs but higher total interest.

4 Calculate Results

Click the "Calculate EMI" button to see your monthly payment, total interest, and overall repayment amount.

Example Calculation:

For a ₹5,00,000 loan at 8.5% interest for 20 years:

  • Monthly EMI: ₹4,343
  • Total Interest: ₹5,42,320
  • Total Payment: ₹10,42,320

Notice how the total interest is even higher than the principal amount with longer tenures!

EMI Formula

EMI = [P × R × (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N-1]

Where:
P = Principal loan amount
R = Monthly interest rate (annual rate/12/100)
N = Loan tenure in months
